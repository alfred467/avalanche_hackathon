# 📜 Kelper (Kenyan Equity Legal Framework)
## A Founder-Friendly, Investor-Safe, and Code-Driven Legal Framework for Kenyan Startups

This document outlines a blueprint for **Bounty 4 (Equity Investing Legal Framework for Kenya)**. It defines the core legal problems, proposes a localized adaptation of startup investment instruments, introduces specific guards against fund siphoning, and details an **NPM-based code system** to make these documents interactive, accessible, and easily generated by non-lawyers.

---

## 1. The Core Legal Problems in Kenya

### A. The "Delaware Import" Mismatch
Most Kenyan startups use US-imported templates like the YC SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) or NVCA documents. These templates fail in Kenya due to:
* **The Companies Act, 2015:** Kenya abolished the concept of "authorized share capital" (replacing it with nominal share capital and share premium accounts). US SAFEs refer to "shares of capital stock," which does not align with Kenyan share classes (e.g., Ordinary Shares, Preference Shares).
* **Registry Bureaucracy (BRS/eCitizen):** Allotting or transferring shares requires formal filing of Form CR2 (Return of Allotment) or Form CR18 (Transfer of Shares) with the Business Registration Service (BRS), paying stamp duty (typically 1% of the value), and obtaining KRA (Kenya Revenue Authority) PINs. US templates assume instant, frictionless digital cap table updates.
* **Capital Gains Tax (CGT) & Stamp Duty:** Conversions and transfers trigger tax events that must be structured carefully under the Kenyan Income Tax Act to avoid unexpected tax liabilities for pre-revenue companies.

### B. The Siphoning Risk
In low-trust or early-stage ecosystems, standard contracts rely on the threat of litigation as a deterrent. In Kenya, this is ineffective because:
* **Court Backlogs:** Dispute resolution in Kenyan commercial courts can take **3 to 5 years** and costs more than the initial investment.
* **Siphoning Mechanisms:** Founders can easily drain capital through inflated salaries, related-party contracts (e.g., hiring a consulting firm owned by a founder's spouse), or transferring Intellectual Property (IP) out of the Kenyan company to a personal or secondary entity.

## 2. The Solution: Kelper-SAFE & Kelper-FOUNDER

To solve these issues, we propose two core documents written in **Plain English**, with built-in **enforceability patches** and **anti-siphoning safeguards**.

### 🌟 Part A: The Kelper-SAFE (Kenyan Simple Agreement for Future Equity)
An investment instrument adapted for the Kenyan Companies Act, 2015, featuring active financial guardrails:

#### 1. Anti-Siphoning Guardrails (The "Kenyan Context" Patch)
* **Founder Compensation Cap:** Founder salaries and expense reimbursements are capped at a specific KES amount (e.g., KES 150,000 per month) until the SAFE converts or a pre-agreed milestone is reached, unless approved by a majority of SAFE holders.
* **Related-Party Transaction Lock:** Any contract or payment to a founder, family member, or entity in which a founder has an interest must be disclosed and approved by the investor.
* **Dual-Signatory Trigger (M-Pesa/Bank):** If the company's bank account or business M-Pesa till processes a single payment exceeding a threshold (e.g., KES 200,000 / $1,500), it requires dual authorization (one founder and one investor-appointed observer, or a pre-approved list of vendors).
* **The IP Lockbox:** Any attempt to transfer, license, or assign the company's intellectual property to a third party or a founder-controlled entity without investor consent is void *ab initio* and constitutes a material breach.
* **Fraud Personal Guarantee:** A specific clause that "lifts the corporate veil." If a founder is found to have siphoned company funds, they lose their limited liability protection under the agreement and become personally liable to refund the investor.

#### 2. Out-of-Court Enforcement (No-Court Arbitration)
* **Fast-Track Med-Arb (30 Days):** Disputes must be referred to a single, pre-agreed local arbitrator (e.g., a technology legal practitioner) and resolved within 30 days.
* **Equitable Lien on Intellectual Property & Assets:** In the event of a siphoning breach or insolvency, the SAFE holder is granted an automatic lien over the company's code repository, domain names, and physical assets, allowing them to take control of these assets immediately without waiting for a court order.

---

### 🌟 Part B: The Kelper-FOUNDER Agreement
A clean, plain-language founder agreement regulating relationships, equity, and IP ownership.

#### 1. Frictionless Vesting & Reverse Vesting (The BRS Workaround)
* **The Problem:** In Kenya, clawing back unvested shares from a departing founder requires them to sign a Form CR18 (Transfer of Shares). If the founder leaves on bad terms, they can refuse to sign, holding the company hostage.
* **The Solution (Power of Attorney):** Upon signing the Kelper-FOUNDER agreement, each founder signs a **limited Power of Attorney (PoA)** and a pre-signed Form CR18 with the share amount left blank. These are held in escrow by the Company Secretary (CPS). If a founder leaves before vesting, the Company Secretary is legally authorized to execute the transfer of the unvested shares back to the treasury or remaining founders for a nominal fee of KES 1.

#### 2. Side-Hustle and Commitment Clause
* In Kenya, side-hustles are common. The agreement contains an explicit "Primary Commitment" clause. Founders must list all existing side businesses. Any new business activity requires the consent of the other founders and investors, ensuring 100% focus on the startup.

#### 3. IP Assignment Deed
* Contains a comprehensive, Kenyan-law-compliant intellectual property assignment clause matching the **Kenyan Copyright Act** and the **Industrial Property Act**.

## 3. The NPM Code System: `k-legal`

Instead of distributing static Word documents, this bounty will feature an **NPM-based interactive legal generator**. This allows non-lawyers to generate, understand, and customize these agreements.

### A. CLI Architecture
Founders and investors can run a single command to start the interactive generator:
```bash
npx k-legal-cli init
```

The CLI guides the user through an interactive setup:
```
? Name of the Kenyan Startup: Safi Tech Limited
? Registered Office Address (Kenya): Nairobi, Westlands, Woodvale Grove
? Founder Names & Equity Splits:
  - Jane Koech (50%)
  - Peter Omondi (50%)
? What is the vesting schedule? 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
? Who is the Company Secretary (CPS)? [Optional] Standard Secretary services
? Enter SAFE Investment Amount (USD or KES): KES 5,000,000
? Enter Valuation Cap: KES 100,000,000
? Anti-Siphoning - Max Founder Salary: KES 200,000/month
? Anti-Siphoning - Dual Signatory Threshold: KES 150,000
```

### B. Interactive Web Preview (`npx k-legal-cli preview`)
Once generated, the system launches a local web server displaying the contract in a **split-screen interactive UI**:
* **Left Panel:** The formal, enforceable Kenyan legal contract.
* **Right Panel (The Plain-English Translator):** Dynamically updates as you hover over clauses.
  * *Example:* Hovering over the *Reverse Vesting* clause displays: *"If Peter leaves the company in Year 1, he gets 0% of his shares. If he leaves in Year 2, he keeps 25%, and the Company Secretary will automatically transfer the other 75% back to Jane using the pre-signed Form CR18 without needing Peter's signature."*
  * *Example:* Hovering over the *Personal Guarantee* clause displays: *"Warning: If you transfer company money to your personal M-Pesa for non-business expenses, the investor can sue you personally, and your personal assets (cars, land) can be seized."*

### C. Output Formats
The system compiles the templates into:
1. **Markdown (`.md`)**: Version-control friendly, easy to diff on GitHub.
2. **PDF (`.pdf`)**: Print-ready and formatted for execution.
3. **JSON Config (`k-legal.json`)**: Stores the metadata of the transaction, allowing easy regeneration or updates in the future.

## 4. Why This Approach is "Top Notch" for the Bounty

1. **Practical Enforceability:** It addresses the actual bottlenecks of the Kenyan registry (BRS) and commercial courts, rather than copying-and-pasting Delaware logic.
2. **Real-world Anti-Fraud Controls:** It addresses the investor's biggest fear in early-stage African markets (money siphoning) by introducing practical banking thresholds and personal fraud liability.
3. **Plain English + Tech Integration:** By wrapping the legal documents in an NPM system, it bridges the gap between law and code. Developers/founders can understand what they are signing, track contract changes via git diffs, and generate execution-ready contracts in seconds.
4. **Kenyan Legal Practitioner Checked:** The system and clauses are mapped to actual sections of the Kenyan Companies Act, 2015, and the Income Tax Act, designed under advice from Kenyan legal practitioners.
