The Problem

Why imported templates fail Kenyan founders

US to KE

Delaware Logic in Nairobi

US SAFE templates reference "authorized shares" and "capital stock" — concepts that do not exist under the Kenyan Companies Act, 2015. The documents are legally meaningless here.

3 to 5 Years

Courts Are Not a Real Option

Standard contracts rely on the threat of court action. In Kenya, commercial litigation takes years and costs more than the original investment. The deterrent does not work.

No Guard

No Siphoning Protection

Founders can drain capital through inflated salaries, related-party contracts, or by moving IP to a personal company. US templates have no guardrails for this reality.

60 Pages

Documents Nobody Reads

Founders sign documents they do not understand. Investors use templates too complex to adapt. The paperwork becomes a liability, not a protection.

The Solution

Written for how Kenya actually works

  • Kenyan Companies Act, 2015 compliant

    Uses correct legal concepts like share allotment, Form CR2 and CR18, and stamp duty — not Delaware terminology.

  • Anti-siphoning clauses built in

    Founder salary caps, M-Pesa dual-signatory thresholds, and a personal fraud guarantee that pierces the corporate veil.

  • 30-day Med-Arb, no courts needed

    Fast-track arbitration under the Kenyan Arbitration Act keeps disputes private and resolved within a month.

  • Plain English throughout

    Every clause has a plain-language translation. A founder can read and understand the entire document without a lawyer present.
