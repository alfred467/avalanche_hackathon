Our Mission

Bridging Kenyan Innovation with Global Capital

Kelper (Kenyan Equity Legal Framework) was built out of a critical necessity. We realized that hundreds of brilliant Kenyan startups were failing not because of bad products, but because of incompatible legal structures. Founders were using copy-pasted American SAFE notes that have no legal standing under the Kenyan Companies Act, 2015.

We built Kelper to create a Cross-Border Legal Bridge. Our platform ensures that every clause is fully anchored in the Constitution of Kenya and the Kenyan Companies Act, while explicitly mirroring US Delaware C-Corp mechanics and International Chamber of Commerce (ICC) standards. This dual compatibility means a startup in Nairobi can confidently sign a Kelper-SAFE with an investor in San Francisco, and both parties know their rights are protected globally and locally.
